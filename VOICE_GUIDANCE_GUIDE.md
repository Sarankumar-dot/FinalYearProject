# 🎙️ Voice-Guided Navigation System for Visually Impaired Users

## Overview

The voice-guided navigation system helps visually impaired students navigate the platform using audio feedback, even if they prefer to use a mouse instead of just keyboard navigation. The system **speaks out loud** to guide them where they want to go and what they can do.

---

## ✨ How Voice Guidance Works

### For Mouse Users

When you **move your mouse and hover over** buttons or form fields, or when you click them, the system **automatically speaks** what the button does and how to interact with it.

**Example:**

- You hover your mouse over a button → System speaks: "Button: Submit Quiz"
- You click a button → System speaks: "Button: Submit Quiz. Activate with Enter"
- You hover over a text field → System speaks: "Email address. Email input. Required field. Example: yourname@example.com"
- You enter a form → System speaks: "Login Form. Form with 2 fields: Email address. Password."

**The Best Part:** You don't need a special mouse or software. Just move your mouse naturally and listen to the audio guidance!

### For Keyboard Users

The same voice guidance works with keyboard navigation, providing verbal descriptions as you Tab through the interface.

---

## 🎙️ Voice Guidance Features

### 1. **Mouse Hover Announcements** ⭐ NEW

When you move your mouse over buttons, links, form fields, or other interactive elements, the system **speaks out loud** to tell you exactly what you're hovering over:

- **Hovers over a button** → Hears: "Button: Next Question"
- **Hovers over a text field** → Hears: "Email address, text input, required"
- **Hovers over a link** → Hears: "Link: View Grades"
- **Hovers over a dropdown** → Hears: "Grade dropdown, select input"

This is the **primary navigation method** for visually impaired users who prefer using a mouse - just move your mouse around and you'll hear everything you can click!

### 2. **Click Announcements**

When you click on elements, they're announced to confirm your action:

- **Click a button** - What the button does
- **Click a link** - Where it goes
- **Click an option** - Which option you selected

### 3. **Automatic Element Announcement** 

When you focus any interactive element (keyboard Tab or mouse), it's announced:

- **Buttons** - What they do and their current state
- **Form fields** - Label, type, and whether required
- **Links** - Where they go
- **Dropdowns** - Number of options
- **Tables** - Structure and headers

### 4. **Page Overview**

Press **Alt + P** to hear:

- Page title
- Main content topic
- Available sections
- Quick navigation summary

### 5. **Menu Navigation**

Press **Alt + M** to hear:

- All navigation menu items
- Number of items available
- Current page information

### 4. **Context-Aware Actions**

Press **Alt + N** to hear:

- What you can do next
- Available buttons and their purposes
- Form fields to complete

## 🎚️ Adjusting Voice Guidance

### Voice Speed

In Accessibility Panel (Alt + A):

1. Look for "Text-to-Speech" section
2. Expand it
3. Use the speed slider: **0.5× (slow) to 2.0× (fast)**
4. Default: 1.0× (normal speed)

### Enable/Disable Features

In Accessibility Panel (Alt + A):

1. Expand "Voice Guidance" section
2. Toggle **"Voice on Mouse Hover"** on/off ⭐ PRIMARY for mouse users
3. Toggle **"Voice Guide Every Action"** on/off
4. Toggle **"Auto-Announce Elements"** on/off

### What Each Feature Does

**Voice on Mouse Hover** (🖱️ recommended for mouse users)
- Speaks when you move your mouse over buttons, fields, links
- Best for: Mouse users navigating intuitively
- Lets you "feel around" with your mouse and hear what's available
- This is the PRIMARY way visually impaired mouse users navigate

**Voice Guide Every Action**
- Speaks when you click buttons or focus form fields
- Best for: Combined mouse + keyboard users
- Confirms your actions with audio feedback
- Works with both clicking and keyboard Tab

**Auto-Announce Elements**
- Speaks form fields and buttons as you navigate with Tab key
- Best for: Keyboard-only users
- Automatic feedback as you move around
- No need to hover or click

## 🔊 Understanding Voice Feedback

### What You'll Hear When Using Mouse Hover

Mouse hover announcements are **short** so you can move to the next element quickly:

```
"Button: Next Question"          ← What you're hovering over
"Email address, text input"      ← Field name and type
"Link: View Your Grades"         ← Link name/destination
"Dropdown, 3 options"            ← Element type and count
```

No long explanations - just the essentials so you know what you're pointing at!

### What Each Announcement Means

#### Form Fields

```
"Email address, email input, required"
```

- **Email address** = The field's label
- **email input** = The type of field
- **required** = Must be filled (may be omitted if optional)

#### Buttons

```
"Button: Submit Quiz"
```

- **Button:** = It's a button
- **Submit Quiz** = What it does

#### Links

```
"Link: View Grades"
```

- **Link:** = It's a clickable link
- **View Grades** = Where it goes / what it does

#### Dropdowns/Selects

```
"Role dropdown. 3 options available. Use arrow keys to select"
```

- **dropdown** = It's a select field
- **3 options** = Number of choices
- **Use arrow keys** = How to interact with it

#### Tables

```
"Table with 4 columns and 5 rows. Column headers: Name, Age, Grade, Status"
```

- **4 columns and 5 rows** = Table dimensions
- **Column headers:** = What each column contains

## 🎯 Tips for Effective Voice Navigation

### 1. **Enable Mouse Hover for Natural Navigation** ⭐

- This is the PRIMARY way to navigate - move your mouse and listen
- Much more intuitive than keyboard navigation if you prefer a mouse
- You're basically "painting" over the page with your mouse and hearing what's there

### 2. **Move Mouse Edge to Edge**

- Move from top-left corner to bottom-right systematically
- This ensures you find everything on the page
- You'll hear each button, field, and link as you pass over it

### 3. **Use Shortcuts for Quick Info**

- **Alt + P** when you first open a page (get overview)
- **Alt + M** if you need to jump to navigation
- **Alt + N** if you're stuck and don't know what to do next
- These **supplement** mouse hover, not replace it

### 4. **Adjust Speech Speed**

- Start at 1.0× (normal)
- If announcements are too fast, lower to 0.8× or 0.7×
- If too slow, increase to 1.2× or 1.5×
- Find your comfortable speed

### 5. **Use Both Mouse and Keyboard**

- Hover with mouse to **discover** what's available
- Click with mouse to **activate**
- Use Tab/Arrows to **fine-tune** selection in dropdowns/forms
- Combine methods for best experience

### 6. **Listen for Announcements**

- Pay attention to what's being spoken
- Note required vs. optional fields
- Listen for error messages about validation

### 5. **Ask for Next Steps**

- When unsure what to do, press **Alt + N**
- System tells you available actions
- Navigate to what you want

### 6. **Enable Both Settings**

- Turn on "Voice Guide Every Action" - speaksbutton/field when clicked
- Turn on "Auto-Announce Elements" - speaks when you Tab to them
- Use both for maximum guidance

## 🆘 Troubleshooting Voice Guidance

### Voice Not Speaking

**Problem:** You click a button but nothing is spoken

**Solutions:**

1. Check volume is not muted
2. Ensure "Voice Guided Navigation" is enabled in settings (Alt + A)
3. Try pressing Alt + H to test voice
4. Check browser supports Web Speech API (Chrome, Edge, Firefox all support it)
5. Refresh page and try again

### Voice Too Slow/Fast

**Problem:** Spoken text is too slow or too fast

**Solutions:**

1. Press Alt + A to open settings
2. Find "Text-to-Speech" section, expand it
3. Adjust speed slider (0.5× to 2.0×)
4. Test with a button to verify new speed
5. Save settings (happen automatically)

### Not Announcing Everything

**Problem:** Some buttons/fields aren't being announced

**Solutions:**

1. Open settings (Alt + A)
2. Find "Voice Guidance" section
3. Enable "Auto-Announce Elements"
4. This will speak every field you focus on
